=== IMPORTANT FEATURES ===
- Automatic billing each time an email is sent and received - under the correct MAT number with an accurate mini narration. Billing for emails sent and received is extremely tedious and time consuming, and as noted above, could be 1000s of emails across various matters, a month. Billing for the big items (day fees, court attendances, meetings, etc.) are fine, but the emails, especially in the litigation space, not so great. 
- Real time tracking of my hours on different matters
- Recordal of fees by team members to track time and fee target progress; being updated on balances outstanding by clients - downloading statements; checking fee targets; bill and unbillable hours; downloading documents / emails / pleadings uploaded thereon ; keeping track on matters.
- Integration with Outlook and T-Drive to ensure seamless tracking. Outlook Ghost Practice "add-ins" are very useful. 
- 1)Full cross-departmental integration: The system must seamlessly integrate all departments (Finance, Conveyancing, Admin, etc.) to eliminate silos and ensure real-time visibility across matters.
2)Centralised document management: The ability to store, access, and manage all documents within the system is essential, with documents linked directly to each matter for ease of reference and compliance.
3)Advanced and customisable reporting: The system should allow the firm to generate tailored reports based on specific requirements, including accurate tracking of active vs inactive files and departmental performance.
4)File lifecycle management: Functionality that allows secretaries to deactivate files once Finance has finalised matters, ensuring cleaner data and more reliable reporting on active matters.
5)Seamless Outlook integration: Direct saving of emails into a matter’s transactional history without unnecessary categorisation steps (e.g., avoiding manual allocation between web, fees, or file notes).
6)Operational efficiency and automation: Reduction of manual processes through automation, improving turnaround times and minimizing administrative workload.
